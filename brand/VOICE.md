# Trueline voice, tone and copy rules

How Trueline sounds. Read this before drafting any customer-facing copy, and before reviewing anything an external partner or AI tool has produced.

Source: *Trueline Marketing Update 2026 Onwards §5 and §14*.

## How Trueline should sound

> Trueline should sound like an **experienced local expert who is proud of the work, calm under pressure and genuinely helpful**. The voice should be warm enough to sell lifestyle, but clear enough to build trust.

## The five voice attributes

| Attribute | How it sounds | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | We know the process and can guide you clearly. | Arrogant claims with no proof. |
| **Human** | We talk about homes, families, weekends and real concerns. | Generic construction jargon. |
| **Premium practical** | Quality matters because it protects your home and your investment. | Luxury language that feels out of reach. |
| **Reassuring** | We explain steps, choices and responsibilities. | Vague promises like "stress free" without process proof. |
| **Local** | We understand Australian weather and local homes. | Copy that could belong to any builder in any country. |

If a sentence doesn't sit inside this table, rewrite it until it does.

## Tone by channel

Different surfaces call for different dials on the same voice.

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Website** | Clear, reassuring, benefit-led. | *Designed for your home, built for Australian conditions, managed from start to finish.* |
| **Social media** | Warm, visual, aspirational but useful. | *This is what a north-facing patio can do for weekend entertaining.* |
| **Sales follow-up** | Helpful, specific, trust-building. | *Here are the next steps, what we handle, and what you can expect from us.* |
| **Reviews / testimonials** | Customer language first. | *Let customers describe relief, pride and confidence in their own words.* |
| **Ads** | Emotion first, proof second, action third. | *Make the backyard the room your family uses most. Book a free in-home quote.* |
| **Internal comms / team** | Direct, friendly, no jargon for jargon's sake. | (See "Internal vs external mode" below.) |
| **Supplier / partner** | Respectful, businesslike, brand-led. | Use the positioning statement verbatim where it adds clarity. |

## Copy rules

These are non-negotiable. Apply them to every draft.

1. **Lead with the customer outcome before the product specification.** Patio = a better Sunday lunch, not a 38mm aluminium beam.
2. **Use proof immediately after emotional claims.** Don't leave a lifestyle statement hanging without a number, a review, a project or a guarantee nearby.
3. **Turn building complexity into customer clarity.** If the customer would have to look it up, rephrase it.
4. **Prefer plain English.** *"We handle council approvals"* beats *"end-to-end regulatory facilitation"* every time.
5. **Use specific lifestyle scenes.** Saturday lunch, school holidays, storm season, morning coffee, caravan protection — concrete beats generic.
6. **Avoid sounding like every other patio builder.** *"Quality workmanship"* is only useful when backed by visible proof.

## Do / Do not

Use this list verbatim when briefing AI tools or external partners — it's the governance core.

### Do

- Use customer-centred language: home, family, shade, comfort, protection, pride, relief, lifestyle.
- Mention trust proof where relevant: experience, completed projects, reviews, warranties, process, local knowledge.
- Explain complex choices simply.
- Show how the product improves the customer's life or protects something they value.
- Keep copy warm, practical, confident and premium-accessible.

### Do not

- Sound like a generic builder or discount shed company.
- Lead with technical specifications unless the audience is already in research mode.
- Use vague claims like *"best quality"* without proof.
- Overuse luxury language that makes the brand feel expensive for the sake of it.
- Let AI create unsupported claims, fake guarantees, fake reviews or inaccurate product details.

## Vocabulary

### Use these

- *Covered outdoor living / covered outdoor space*
- *Custom-designed* (not "bespoke" — too precious)
- *Designed for your home / built for Australian conditions*
- *Your family / your home / your space* — second-person, possessive
- *We handle / we guide / we manage* — paired with the thing customers fear having to do themselves
- *Proven / experienced / local*
- Product nouns are fine: patios, carports, verandahs, decks, insulated roofs, flyovers, louvres

### Avoid these in customer-facing copy

- *Solutions* (consultancy-speak)
- *Cutting-edge*, *best-in-class*, *world-class* (clichés without proof)
- *Synergy / leverage / utilise* — replace with simpler words
- Construction jargon for outsiders: *footings, purlins, eaves rectification, structural framing* — fine in technical sections, not in marketing leads
- Aggressive sales language: *act now, limited time, don't miss out*
- *Stress free* on its own — only use when you can immediately show the process proof behind it

## Worked examples

### Customer enquiry reply

✅ *"Thanks for the enquiry — really nice to hear from a Hamilton homeowner. From your photos, you've got a great north-facing position to work with. We'll bring a couple of design options to the appointment so you can see what a covered space could look like against your existing roofline, and we'll walk you through how we handle the council side. Looking forward to meeting you."*

❌ *"Thank you for your enquiry. Please find attached our standard brochure. We offer industry-leading patio solutions and pride ourselves on quality workmanship. A consultant will be in touch."*

### Marketing headline

✅ *"Cover the Sunday lunch."*

❌ *"Industry-leading patio solutions for the modern Queensland family."*

### Social post about a finished build

✅ *"Finished this one in Hamilton last week. Spotted gum decking, white-on-white frame, full insulated roof. Owner sent us a photo of the family eating dinner out there the next night — that's the bit we love."*

❌ *"Project completion: Hamilton patio. 6.2x4.8m. Aluminium frame, polycarbonate roof. Installation time: 5 days. ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐"*

### Quote / proposal intro

✅ *"Thanks for letting us into your project. Here's what we'd build for you, what it would cost, and what we'd handle along the way."*

❌ *"Please find attached our proposal for your consideration as per our recent discussions."*

### Review reply

✅ *"Thanks Sarah — really chuffed your family is getting use out of the new space. The insulated roof was the right call for that west-facing position. Enjoy summer under it."*

❌ *"Thank you for your 5-star review. Customer satisfaction is our top priority."*

## Internal vs external mode

Trueline communications operate in **two visual modes** (see `BRAND.md` for the visual rule). The voice mostly stays the same, but the dial shifts:

- **External** (customer-facing, agency, supplier, press): full voice — warm, aspirational, proof-led. Use everything in this file.
- **Internal** (team comms, ops notes, HR letters, internal slides): keep the voice, drop the marketing language. Direct, friendly, no jargon, decisions and next steps clear. Don't pitch the brand at colleagues; just write like a sensible Trueline person would speak in a meeting.

## The voice test (when you're unsure)

Read the draft and ask:

> *"Would a homeowner reading this feel like Trueline gets them, or like Trueline is just trying to sell them something?"*

If the latter, soften. If you can't tell, push it toward the customer-language side of the line.
